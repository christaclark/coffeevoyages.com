BRAZIL

We organize trips to go visit rural, organic coffee farms in exotic places. Our expected group size is 10 people. This trip will include a visit to 3 coffee farms, a coffee warehouse and mill, on site cupping, a class in preparing common foods of Minas Gerais, Santos Coffee Museum tour, a visit to Tiradentes, historical town in Minas Gerais, and will end with a gastronomical tour in São Paulo. This trip is hosted by Casa Brazil.

Coffee Voyages is made up of a group of coffee lovers and adventure seekers who want to take their love of coffee to a whole new level. If you have ever dreamed of going to rural locations to see, touch, and taste the purest coffee then our trips are a perfect way to spend your next vacation!

Brazilian Team
Joel Shuler

Joel is the owner of Casa Brasil Coffees and Little City Coffee Roasters. His areas of specialization are in coffee sensory analysis and post-harvest processing. He is a Q grade assistant instructor, holds the Port of Santos Coffee Classification License and is currently pursuing a master’s degree at the Universidade Federal de Lavras (UFLA) in coffee post-harvest processing. His translation of Handbook of Post-Harvest Coffee Technology has sold in more than 20 countries and he recently co-authored a chapter on coffee production for the Royal Society of Chemistry. Joel has served on numerous international coffee juries and in 2014 represented the Brazilian Specialty Coffee Association (BSCA) in the United States.

Lisiane Shuler

A native of Belo Horizonte, Minas Gerais, Lisiane is co-owner of Casa Brasil Coffees and Little City Coffee Roasters. A Licensed Master Social Worker (LMSW), she completed her master’s in Social Work from the University of Texas at Austin. Through Casa Brasil, Coffee Kids, and other charities Lisiane has worked with coffee communities in Acatenango, Guatemala; San Ramón, Costa Rica, and Poço Fundo, Brazil.

Paula Magalhães Paiva

Paula was raised on Fazenda Recanto, an award-winning and Rainforest Alliance certified coffee farm that has been in her family since 1896. In 2012 she organized a delegation of coffee co-op presidents from Minas Gerais to represent Brazil at the Trieste Espresso Expo as well as meetings with Illycaffe. A recent graduate from Centro Universitário Una in International Business, Paula has returned to Fazenda Recanto where she is developing a line of roasted coffees. Fazenda Recanto has won the Brazilian Rainforest Alliance competition for best coffee, and the farm is a model for sustainability, with 38% of the farm dedicated to preserving the native Mata Atlântica forest.
